# Attribution

## Methods

ProspectMap implements the Weights-of-Evidence mineral-prospectivity-mapping method and its honest-validation
machinery. The methods are attributable to:

- **Bonham-Carter, G.F., Agterberg, F.P. & Wright, D.F. (1989).** Weights of evidence modelling: a new approach to
  mapping mineral potential. *Geol. Survey Canada Paper 89-9*, 171-183.
- **Bonham-Carter, G.F. (1994).** *Geographic Information Systems for Geoscientists: Modelling with GIS.* Pergamon,
  ch. 9 - the W+/W-/contrast, the studentized contrast, the success/prediction-rate curves, the CI tests.
- **Agterberg, F.P. & Cheng, Q. (2002).** Conditional Independence Test for Weights-of-Evidence Modeling. *Natural
  Resources Research*, 11(4), 249-255 - the new omnibus test.
- **Agterberg, F.P. & Bonham-Carter, G.F. (1990).** Deriving weights of evidence from geoscience contour maps for the
  prediction of discrete events. *22nd APCOM*, 381-395 - the WofE -> logistic-regression bridge.
- **Chung, C.-J. & Fabbri, A.G. (2003).** Validation of spatial prediction models for landslide hazard mapping.
  *Natural Hazards*, 30(3), 451-472 - the success/prediction-rate capture curves.
- **Roberts, D.R. et al. (2017).** Cross-validation strategies for data with temporal, spatial, hierarchical, or
  phylogenetic structure. *Ecography*, 40(8), 913-929 - spatial block CV / the inflation.
- **Carranza, E.J.M. (2009).** *Geochemical Anomaly and Mineral Prospectivity Mapping in GIS.* Elsevier.
- **Lawley, C.J.M. et al. (2022).** Data-driven prospectivity modelling of sediment-hosted Zn-Pb mineral systems.
  *Ore Geology Reviews*, 141, 104635 - the real Zn-Pb prospectivity dataset (the documented next-step data).

Full bibliography in `frontend/src/data/citations.ts` and the in-app Methodology page.

## Data

All study areas are **synthetic** - smooth value-noise fields (geostatistical in spirit, not variogram-controlled)
with planted per-layer weights + deposits rejection-sampled on a known latent prospectivity (fixed count per case),
generated by
`frontend/src/mpm/synth.ts` + the `gen_train.mjs` sampler. No real geoscience data is used or redistributed. The
`C-NEGATIVE`, `C-CIVIOLATE`, `C-RECOVER` and `C-SATURATE` cases are the known-ground-truth controls.

Real open datasets are the documented next step, each redistributable under its own licence: USGS (MRDS, NURE-HSSR,
the Lawley-2022 Zn-Pb ScienceBase grids) are **US-Government public domain**; Geoscience Australia national geophysical
grids + OZMIN are **CC-BY 4.0**; the AlphaEarth Foundations Satellite Embedding is **CC-BY 4.0** ("produced by Google
and Google DeepMind").
